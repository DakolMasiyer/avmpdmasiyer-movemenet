Campaign Platform

Vision &
8-Pillar Agenda

Eight pillars. Eight commitments. Grounded in the real and urgent needs of Mangu/Bokkos Federal Constituency — not borrowed from another constituency's playbook.

The Primary Narrative

"A disciplined leader returning home
to serve and secure his people."

35 years defending Nigeria. The same discipline. The same commitment. Now directed at Mangu/Bokkos.

1

🛡️ Security & Lasting Peace

FOUNDATION PILLAR — Everything Else Depends on This

Security is not one item on the agenda. It is the condition under which every other item becomes possible. Farmer-herder conflict has cost Mangu/Bokkos lives, livelihoods, and decades of development.

  • Advocacy for increased federal security presence in the constituency
  • Structured dialogue platforms between farming and herding communities
  • Community-based intelligence and early warning systems
  • Partnership with traditional rulers and faith leaders on conflict resolution
  • Push for demarcation and protection of agricultural zones
AVM Masiyer's Advantage

"He has operated at the highest levels of Nigeria's security architecture. He does not need to be briefed on how security works — he helped build it."

2

🌾 Farming & Local Business

ECONOMIC PILLAR — Feeding the Constituency, Feeding the Nation

Mangu/Bokkos is one of Plateau State's most productive agricultural zones. Its farmers deserve federal investment, market access, and protection — not just acknowledgement.

  • Advocacy for smallholder farmers' inclusion in federal agricultural programmes
  • Market access road infrastructure connecting farming communities
  • Post-harvest storage and processing facility advocacy
  • Linkage to NIRSAL and CBN agricultural financing schemes
  • Support for agribusiness entrepreneurs and cooperatives
The Commitment

"When I say I will support our farmers, I mean I will use the National Assembly's power to redirect federal resources to where they are needed most — here."

3

🎓 Education & Healthcare

HUMAN DEVELOPMENT — The Foundation of Every Generation

Quality schools and functional health facilities in every ward. Not aspirational language — specific, ward-by-ward accountability for delivery.

  • Advocacy for federal constituency project funding to rehabilitate schools
  • Push for federal health facility upgrades in underserved wards
  • Maternal and child health programme access for rural communities
  • Scholarship advocacy for Mangu/Bokkos students in federal institutions
  • Emergency healthcare access connectivity
The Standard

"No child in Mangu/Bokkos should travel more than reasonable distance to reach a school or clinic. That is the standard we are setting."

4

💼 Youth Empowerment

RETENTION PILLAR — Keep the Future at Home

Young people leave Mangu/Bokkos because there is not enough to hold them here. The goal is to change that — not by asking them to stay with nothing, but by building the infrastructure of opportunity.

  • Skills acquisition programmes in technical, digital, and agribusiness fields
  • Federal SMEDAN and NYSC programme partnerships for the constituency
  • Entrepreneurship support fund access and mentorship networks
  • Diaspora connection programme for knowledge and investment transfer
  • Youth representation in constituency office decision-making
The Message

"I do not want our young people to have to leave to find a future. The goal is to make Mangu/Bokkos a place where opportunity exists — not somewhere you have to escape from."

5

🛣️ Economic & Physical Development

INFRASTRUCTURE PILLAR — Roads, Utilities, Federal Investment

Roads are how farmers get to market. Electricity is how businesses stay open at night. Infrastructure is not a luxury — it is the physical expression of whether a constituency is valued.

  • Constituency project funding targeted at road rehabilitation across both LGAs
  • Rural electrification advocacy for underserved communities
  • Water infrastructure programme access
  • Market infrastructure rehabilitation and expansion
  • Transparent constituency project tracking and public reporting
The Accountability Pledge

"Every constituency allocation I secure will be publicly tracked. The community will know what was received, what was spent, and what was delivered."

6

👩‍👧 Women, Elderly & Vulnerable

INCLUSION PILLAR — No One Left Behind

Women make up more than half of this constituency. The elderly built it. The vulnerable depend on it. None of them should be an afterthought in federal policy.

  • Advocacy for constituency inclusion in federal women's empowerment programmes
  • Social investment programme access for elderly and disabled constituents
  • Market women's economic inclusion in federal MSMEs programmes
  • Women's representation in constituency engagement structures
  • Advocacy for federal social protection nets for vulnerable households
The Commitment

"When I say this movement is for everyone, I mean it begins with those who have been most overlooked."

7

🥁 Cultural Heritage

IDENTITY PILLAR — Who We Are Is Worth Preserving and Sharing

The Mwaghavul, Challa, and the many peoples of Mangu/Bokkos carry a cultural identity that is rich, distinctive, and worth celebrating — not just internally, but at the national and global level.

  • Advocacy for cultural heritage funding through federal arts and culture bodies
  • Support for Mangu/Bokkos festivals and cultural events on the national calendar
  • Documentation and preservation of indigenous languages and oral traditions
  • Connecting constituency cultural identity with tourism development
  • Youth engagement in cultural preservation — making heritage relevant
The Vision

"Our culture is not a relic. It is a living, breathing identity that deserves to be seen, heard, and celebrated — at home and far beyond Plateau State."

8

📢 A Voice at the National Stage

REPRESENTATION PILLAR — Heard When It Matters

Mangu/Bokkos has been present in the National Assembly but not always heard. The difference between a representative and a voice is whether they are listened to when it counts.

  • Committee assignments targeted at constituencies' priority areas: agriculture, defence, infrastructure
  • Active legislative presence — bills, motions, and amendments that name Mangu/Bokkos
  • Regular constituency briefings on federal budget and policy developments
  • Cross-party coalition building on Plateau State and Middle Belt issues
  • Accessible constituency office with real responsiveness
The Differentiator

"The man who managed the logistics of the entire Nigerian Air Force will have no difficulty navigating the machinery of the National Assembly to deliver for this constituency."
